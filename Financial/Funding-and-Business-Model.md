> **Significant Change Log**
> A record of meaningful changes to this document — entries made when the content shifts in a way that would affect how a reader interprets decisions made against it. Routine updates (correcting a date, adding a new entry to a register) do not require a log entry. A log entry is warranted when: the document's overall picture has changed, a core definition or principle has shifted, organizational context that drove earlier decisions has been replaced, or a reader a year from now would need to know that this document meant something different before this date.
>
> When writing an entry, capture not just what changed but what the document reflected *before* — so the log itself is recoverable context, not just a changelog.

## 4. Organizational Drivers

> What the organization is actually optimizing for right now. This is distinct from strategy — it is the financial and operational logic that determines how tradeoffs are made. Two organizations with identical strategies can make opposite decisions if their drivers differ.

### 4.1 Primary Driver

**Current primary driver**: [Select one — be honest]
- [ ] **Revenue growth** — top-line expansion is the primary metric; profitability is secondary
- [ ] **Customer growth** — logo count and market penetration matter more than revenue per customer
- [ ] **Proof of concept** — demonstrating that the model works; early customer validation is the goal
- [ ] **Market exploration** — learning what customers value before committing to a defined model
- [ ] **Retention and expansion** — growing within the existing customer base; new logos are secondary
- [ ] **Profit growth** — margin expansion and earnings are the primary focus
- [ ] **Shareholder value / Exit preparation** — optimizing for a transaction (acquisition, IPO, or recapitalization)
- [ ] **Mission fulfillment** — outcomes for constituents take priority over financial metrics
- [ ] **Operational efficiency** — cost reduction and process improvement are the dominant focus
- [ ] **Stability and sustainability** — maintaining a healthy, durable business without aggressive growth

**Driver narrative:**
[Explain what this driver means in practice — how it shows up in decisions. e.g., Revenue growth is the current primary driver. The board has set a target of 2x ARR within 24 months. This means sales capacity and pipeline expansion take priority over margin improvement, and product decisions are evaluated primarily on their ability to accelerate new customer acquisition or reduce churn.]

### 4.2 Secondary Drivers

[List 1–2 secondary drivers and briefly describe how they interact with the primary driver. e.g., Customer success and retention is a strong secondary driver — the growth target is net revenue, so churn directly offsets new bookings. This creates healthy tension between the sales motion (volume) and the CS motion (quality).]

**What drives this horizon:**

### 5.2 Exit Orientation

**Exit orientation**: [Select one]
- [ ] **No defined exit** — building for the long term; no transaction planned or anticipated
- [ ] **Acquisition-oriented** — building toward a strategic acquisition; actively or passively positioning
- [ ] **IPO-oriented** — building toward a public offering; timeline and readiness are active considerations
- [ ] **PE recapitalization** — current or anticipated private equity ownership with defined hold period
- [ ] **Founder buyout / MBO** — management acquisition of investor stakes
- [ ] **Succession / Transfer** — ownership transition to family, employees, or another structure
- [ ] **Mission preservation** — structure is designed to protect mission over financial return (e.g., benefit corporation, nonprofit)

**Exit narrative:**
[Describe what is known, intended, or likely. Be as honest as the context allows. e.g., The current investor base expects a liquidity event within 4–6 years. Acquisition by a larger platform player is the most likely outcome given our market position and product footprint. An IPO is possible but not the current plan. This shapes our emphasis on clean financials, documented processes, and a scalable org structure — all of which increase acquirer confidence.]
