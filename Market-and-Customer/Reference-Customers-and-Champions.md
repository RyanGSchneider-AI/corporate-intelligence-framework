# Reference Customers and Champions

> A living record of strategically important customer accounts and the individual champions within them. This artifact captures accounts with value beyond standard commercial terms — and the specific people whose advocacy, co-development participation, or organizational influence makes that value real.
>
> **A reference customer is an account. A champion is a person.** Both matter. Accounts churn when champions leave, and champions become your most powerful sales asset when they move to a new organization. This document tracks both.
>
> **Governance note:** Records here are governed by executed agreements and the terms of any reference, co-development, or pilot arrangement. Changes to relationship status, contract terms, or strategic classification should be reflected here promptly.

> **Significant Change Log**
> A record of meaningful changes to this document — entries made when the content shifts in a way that would affect how a reader interprets decisions made against it. Routine updates (correcting a date, adding a new entry to a register) do not require a log entry. A log entry is warranted when: the document's overall picture has changed, a core definition or principle has shifted, organizational context that drove earlier decisions has been replaced, or a reader a year from now would need to know that this document meant something different before this date.
>
> When writing an entry, capture not just what changed but what the document reflected *before* — so the log itself is recoverable context, not just a changelog.

## 1. Classification Criteria

> Not every customer belongs in this document — only those with strategic importance beyond standard commercial terms. Use the criteria below to determine inclusion.

**Include an account if it meets one or more of the following:**

| Criterion | Description |
|---|---|
| **Reference value** | Willing to serve as a public reference, case study, or testimonial — actively supports sales and marketing |
| **Co-development partner** | Active participant in product direction, beta programs, or feature development |
| **Pilot customer** | First adopter of a new product, feature, or market expansion |
| **Strategic foothold** | Provides access to a new segment, geography, or distribution channel beyond their commercial value alone |
| **Revenue anchor** | Top-tier account by revenue whose retention materially affects business performance |

## 3. Champion Directory

> Individual champions within strategic accounts — the people whose advocacy, organizational influence, or product knowledge makes the relationship valuable. Champions are tracked separately from accounts because they move, get promoted, and change organizations.

> **A champion who leaves their account is not a lost asset.** They often become a buyer or advocate at their next organization. Track departures as pipeline signals.

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

| [Name] | [Company] | [e.g., Director of Finance] | [e.g., Co-development partner] | [e.g., Product co-designer / Beta program lead] | [Internal owner name] | [High / Medium / Low] | YYYY-MM-DD | |

**Champion type definitions:**
- **Executive sponsor** — Senior leader with organizational authority who has publicly committed to the relationship
- **Product advocate** — Power user with deep product knowledge who promotes internally and externally
- **Referral source** — Actively introduces the organization to prospects in their network
- **Co-designer** — Participates in product development — user research, beta programs, feature feedback
- **Public voice** — Willing to speak at events, participate in case studies, or engage with press and analysts

## 5. Relationship Health Register

> A consolidated view of relationship health across all strategic accounts. Updated quarterly. Flags accounts that need attention before a renewal or reference request reveals the problem.

| Organization | Classification | Renewal Date | Health Signal | Risk Factors | Action Required | Owner | Last Reviewed |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Company] | [Reference / Co-dev / Pilot / Anchor] | YYYY-MM-DD | [Strong / Stable / At Risk / Unknown] | [e.g., Champion departed / Support escalation unresolved / Usage declining] | [e.g., Executive check-in / CS review / QBR] | [Name] | YYYY-MM-DD |

**Health signal definitions:**
- **Strong** — Active engagement, high satisfaction, champion relationship intact, reference activities ongoing
- **Stable** — Normal usage, no active issues, renewal unlikely to be contested
- **At Risk** — One or more risk factors present — champion departure, open escalation, declining usage, or competitor activity known
- **Unknown** — Insufficient contact or data to assess — escalate to relationship owner immediately
